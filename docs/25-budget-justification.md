# 25 — Budget Justification

## Requested Amount

Up to $10,000 (Team1 Mini Grant maximum).

## Allocation

| Category | Amount | Justification |
|---|---|---|
| Development time (8–10 weeks, part-time) | $8,000 | Covers design, implementation, and testing of all six MVP checks (Document 19), the CLI, and the reporting layer, at a part-time pace consistent with concurrent coursework and community commitments. |
| Beta tester incentives | $1,000 | A small incentive for 5–10 real builders to run the tool against their own deployments during Weeks 7–8 and report false positives/negatives — this feedback loop is the primary validation mechanism for the health-score formula (Document 10) and the check accuracy claims generally. |
| Infrastructure (RPC provider costs for CI/testnet checks, CI minutes, domain) | $500 | Ongoing costs to keep CI running and to test against testnet/mainnet RPC endpoints beyond free-tier limits. |
| Contingency | $500 | Buffer for the Week 0 risk identified in Document 22 (unverified assumptions requiring rework) and general schedule slippage. |

## Why Not More, Why Not Less

The scope in Document 19 (MVP) is deliberately bounded to what is achievable by one developer, part-time, in 8–10 weeks — the budget reflects that scope directly rather than being sized to the grant maximum independent of actual planned work. A smaller grant would not meaningfully reduce the core development allocation, since the check implementations themselves are the majority of the effort regardless of funding level; it would primarily cut the beta-incentive and infrastructure lines, which would weaken the validation loop this proposal considers important (Document 21/22).

## Financial Accountability

Milestone-based reporting (Document 26) ties each budget tranche to a concrete, checkable deliverable rather than time elapsed — consistent with Team1's expected grant reporting structure.
